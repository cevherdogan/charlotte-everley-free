Founded in 1936, the TE Historical Society is the longest-running local history organization on the Upper Main Line. With robust digital archives and over 11 feet of manuscripts, it preserves everything from schoolhouse ledgers to family letters and local deeds. Membership is optional, but beneficial for deeper access and permissions.

  • Public Access: Digital journals, maps, and archives are freely available online.
  • In-Person Visits: Some materials like estate maps and diaries require an appointment.
  • Membership Perks: Mailed journals, access to member events, support for research requests.
  • Permissions: Personal-use copying is allowed; redistribution requires formal approval.

üé≠ Valley Forge Music Fair: A Local Cultural Gem

Opened in 1955, the Valley Forge Music Fair brought Broadway-caliber shows to Devon, first under a circus-style tent and later in a 2,750-seat theater. Its legacy includes plays like Guys and Dolls, Gypsy, and performances by John Denver and Tony Bennett. The Fair closed in 1996 but lives on in memory and TEHS documentation.
