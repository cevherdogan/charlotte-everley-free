1. Distance & Directions

From **387 Devonshire Rd**, the drive to Jug Hollow Road is approximately 11 miles west via PA-23 and Gulph Rd—about a 20-minute journey. This puts you at the heart of Valley Forge’s wooded edges and Revolutionary-era scenery.

2. Scenic Experience

Jug Hollow Road is a gently narrowing lane that curves through Valley Creek’s tributary hollow. With towering sycamores and mossy foundations, each turn offers a meadow view over old encampment zones. In spring, mountain laurels bloom; in autumn, fiery leaves blaze.

3. Hidden Trails & Legal Access

Just off Yellow Springs Rd—connected to Jug Hollow—you’ll find a quiet access point to the **Horse‑Shoe Trail**, a 140‑mile public hiking route extending from Valley Forge to the Appalachian Trail [oai_citation:0‡Trailforks](https://www.trailforks.com/trails/horseshoe-trail-209710/?utm_source=chatgpt.com). Entry is legal via a marked pull-off at Brightside Farm (Yellow Springs & Hollow Rd) [oai_citation:1‡Charlestown Township](https://charlestown.pa.us/parks_horse_shoe_trail.aspx?utm_source=chatgpt.com). From here, an easy woodland path leads to Mount Misery and creek-side boardwalks.
